SDR Categories Explained

These definitions are guidelines for organizing radios on SDR-Specs.com. Models can straddle categories based on configuration or use case. Prices are typical street prices (USD) for reference only.

Summary at a Glance

Category Typical Price RX/TX Inst. Bandwidth Typical ADC Example
Entry-Level $20–$150 RX (most) ~2–10 MHz 8–12-bit RTL-SDR v3
Mid-Tier $150–$500 RX or RX/TX ~10–20+ MHz 12–14-bit HackRF One
Professional $500–$5,000+ RX/TX ~40–200+ MHz 14–16-bit Ettus USRP B210
Military/Research $10,000+ RX/TX (multi-channel) ~200 MHz to GHz+ High-end mixed-signal + FPGA Ettus USRP X410

Category Details

Entry-Level SDRs

  • Definition: Low-cost SDRs aimed at beginners, scanning, and general experimentation.
  • Traits: USB dongle form factor, RX-only (usually), narrower bandwidth, simpler front-ends, bus-powered.
  • Typical Use: Broadcast/FM/airband/weather sats, trunking, ADS-B (with add-ons), learning DSP.
  • Example: RTL-SDR v3, SDRplay RSP1A, Airspy Mini.

Mid-Tier SDRs

  • Definition: More capable hobbyist/engineering radios; often add TX, better clocks/filters, wider bandwidth.
  • Traits: 12–14-bit converters, 10–20+ MHz bandwidth, improved shielding and stability.
  • Typical Use: Protocol exploration, replay/Tx experiments (where legal), better HF/VHF/UHF work.
  • Example: HackRF One, ADALM-Pluto, Airspy HF+ Discovery.

Professional SDRs

  • Definition: Commercial/engineering-grade radios for development, monitoring, and deployment.
  • Traits: Higher dynamic range, precise clocks (GPSDO options), robust APIs, enclosure/rack options.
  • Typical Use: Wireless R&D, spectrum surveys, production test, custom PHY work.
  • Example: Ettus USRP B200/B210, LimeSDR (upper entry to pro), SDRplay RSPdx (RX-focused).

Military/Research SDRs

  • Definition: High-end, multi-channel, phase-coherent systems for defense, EW, SATCOM, and deep research.
  • Traits: GHz-class bandwidths, FPGA acceleration, timing/sync (GPSDO/OCXO), ruggedized options.
  • Typical Use: EW/CUAS, passive/active radar research, multi-antenna arrays, field labs.
  • Example: Ettus USRP X410, NI/Ettus N320/N321, Per Vices Cyan family.

Notes: Bandwidth, dynamic range, and price bands are indicative. Always check each model’s datasheet for exact specs and options.
